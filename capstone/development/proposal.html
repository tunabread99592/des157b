Capstone Proposal

Topic

I want to focus on the immediacy of climate change, including one of my biggest fears as a middle-class Californian: what if the power went out during a heat wave? I remember it happening before, and people were panicking under short-term circumstances. I'm specifically inspired by my mother, who has even less resistance to heat than I do and flees to the coast as soon as it hits the nineties.

Then I realized that I didn't understand how power outages worked. I went down a rabbit hole, researching the locations of power surges and the roles of distribution lines. Do office buildings receive power priority over suburban areas? How do towers even collapse under pressure? This idea started to turn into a plausible future.

Identify Lenses

Since this is a more factual, research-based project, I'm relying on data visualization and challenging mental models. The mental models I want to challenge are our reliance on back-up plans. What if the most dramatic effects hit at the same time? My dystopian future is imminent, and combines the instances of the world's worst heat waves. I want to include the melted streets of India, the warped bridges of the Pacific West Coast, the wildfires of Southern California, and the civil unrest of 1977 New York City.

In terms of data visualization, I want to compare your answers to those who have already played; but this time, the percentages of other players' choices affect your changces of survival in the end. Your goal is to pick the least popular paths in order to successfully escape. This will keep players coming back to see if the results have changed.

Possible Features

I'm aiming for a cinematic experience. I want to utilize the parallax scrolling effect, so your journey in your car is bordered by a changing environmental scene. I'm imagining minimal, geometric shapes to mimic the ending credits of action films, increasing the sense of urgency and desperation. This would also contrast the moving particle effects I want to pull from Javascript libraries.

The act of picking an answer will be simple, but maybe each section will have a timer to increase the urgency of surviving. I also want to time-out my descriptors, similar to "Judgement" example. It's interesting how the simple presentation of sentences enhances the situational drama.

Challenges

I am unsure how effective the "Percentage of Survival" conclusion will be. Mathmatically, I assume that I would take the opposite percentage of the chosen category, add them all together, then multiply by the amount of questions, but I'll have to conduct a couple test-runs to see if it makes sense.

I also don't know if my crafted story, with historical references, will seem dystopian enough. The likelihood of this event happening was my solution to a more interesting, immersive presentation of data. I went in circles trying to think of a tech wipeout scenario instead, but it was a lot less straightforward.

Target Audience

My target audience is the older, settled working class, from early 30s to late 40s. It's a time when we assume some sense of security, but outside forces are unavoidable. I think it's interesting that every generation witnesses at least one global disaster. Even though I consider COVID ours, there is a daunting presence that something else is on the rise. If it doesn't happen to us now, it will happen soon.

The middle ages are also a time of mundane: in complacency, routines, established locations and reactionary measures. However, they are still at the crux between taking care of the next generation and the last. Having that weight thrust upon them, plus the happenings of the world, is a cruel and likely scenario.
